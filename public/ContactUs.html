Frequently Asked Questions



FAQ

Training and Placement Cell plays a crucial role in helping students with their career development by connecting them with potential employers and facilitating the placement process. The cell works with companies to arrange campus placement drives, and provides support to students during the selection process.

FAQ

TPC helps students with their career development by providing them with resources and opportunities to meet potential employers and showcase their skills. The cell works with companies to arrange campus placement drives, and provides students with training and guidance on how to prepare for placement, including resume building, and more.

FAQ Query

TPC selects companies for campus placement based on a number of factors, including the company's reputation, the size of the company, the nature of the company's business, and more. The cell also considers the needs and interests of the students and the demands of the job market.

FAQ Query

The different ways of recruiting students are:

  • On-campus recruiting: Companies come to the campus and participate in job fairs, information sessions, and other recruitment events to meet students and present their organization.
  • Direct Hiring: Companies reach out to the placement cell to directly recruit students for full-time positions.
  • Social media and online platforms: Companies use social media platforms, such as LinkedIn or email, to connect with placement cells and promote job opportunities for students.
